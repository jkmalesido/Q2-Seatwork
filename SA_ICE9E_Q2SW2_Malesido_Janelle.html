DOG BREEDS


Image Breed Description
Golden Retriever The Golden Retriever is an ideal family dog because it is lively, loyal, and particulary friendly. It likes to be around people and it is good with children. The Golden Retriever was originally bred in Scotland between 1840 and 1890. The dog was bred as a hunting dog that was suited for Scotland's wet climate. The Golden Retriever's ancestors include the Yellow Retriever, the Tweed Water Spaniel (extinct), the Irish Setter, and the bloodhound. Today, the dog is used as a guide dog, tracker dog, and therapy dog.
Chihuahua Chihuahuas have a super-size personality. It knows what it wants and goes after it with single-minded determination. Although it is small, it is known as an excellent watchdog. Chihuhuas also tend to be high-strung and prone to nipping, snapping, and even biting when frightened or threatened, or when defending his people or territory.
Poodle Poodles are known to have beautiful hairdos and are the best show dogs for their elegant appearance and innate performance ability. Like Golden Retrievers, they also make very affectionate family dogs. Poodles are usually purebred, but you can always find them in rescue shelters, often mixed with other breeds such as Maltese. Moreover, these dogs are very intelligent and are easy to train. They need to be kept busy and too much free time can leave them with destructive behavior.
Shih Tzu Shih Tzus are small but sturdy dogs with lush, long, double-hair coats. They are alert, confident, playful, and courageous, making them a favorite amongst toy dog enthusiasts. Its petite size makes it suitable for small living spaces. This breed has a long, ancient history as lap dogs to nobles. The Shih Tzu is considered a brachycephalic breed because of its head shape and short, "smooshed" face.
Boxer Boxers are loyal, affectionate, intelligent, good-looking, and has great work ethic. Sometimes silly, but always courageous, Boxers have been one of America's most popular dog breeds for a long time. Boxers move like the athletes they are named for: smooth and graceful, with a powerful forward thrust. Their dark brown eyes and wrinkled forehead gives the face an alert, curious look. Boxers are also known to have a patient and protective nature, making them good watchdogs or family guardians.
French Bulldog The French Bulldog typically has large, erect 'bat' ears, a big, square head, heavy wrinkles, and an extremely short nose. It is one of the world's most popular small-dog breeds, especially among city dwellers. They are known as playful, alert, and adaptable. They get on well with other animals and enjoy making new friends.
Basset Hound This low-slung and low-key hound can sometimes be stubborn, but is always charming. The Basset Hound stands no higher than 14 inches at the shoulder but, with its remarkably heavy bone, powerful little legs, and massive paws, it possesses big-dog strength and stamina. Bassets are famous for a large, domed head that has extremely long, velvety ears, mournful eyes, and a wrinkled brow. Although they are not that affectionate, they are steadfastly loyal.
Greyhound Greyhounds are extremely affectionate so they make excellent companions for all kinds of people. These dogs are rarely aggressive but sensitive, requiring patience and understanding. They are known for their muscular long lengs, narrow, streamlined body, and short, smooth coat.
Great Dane Also known as the mighty "Apollo of Dogs," the Great Dane is an easygoing dog. As tall as 32 inches at the shoulder, Danes tower over most other dogs and are taller than most people. They have a gentle and sweet nature, but make excellent watchdogs. Great Danes are people pleasers who make friends easily and are patient with children.
Border Collie The Border Collie is an energetic dog known to be a bright workaholic. They are athletic, medium-sized herders standing 18 to 22 inches at the shoulder. Border Collies are among the dog breed's most agile, balanced, and durable canines. Their intelligence, athleticism, and trainability have a perfect outlet in agility training.
